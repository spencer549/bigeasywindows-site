Covington sits at the heart of St. Tammany Parish, one of the fastest-growing areas in Louisiana. The mix of older established neighborhoods near the Bogue Falaya River and newer construction along Highway 190 means we see a wide range of window projects here, from replacing original wood windows in 1970s ranch homes to supplying full window packages for new builds.

Why Covington Homes Need Quality Windows

The Northshore gets direct exposure to Gulf weather systems that track across the lake. Hurricane Ida in 2021 brought sustained winds well above Category 1 strength to St. Tammany Parish, and the homes that held up best were those with properly installed, impact-rated windows and doors.

Beyond storm season, the humidity and heat load on the Northshore is comparable to the rest of south Louisiana. Modern windows with Low-E glass and argon fill cut the solar heat gain that drives summer cooling costs, and tight weatherstripping eliminates the drafts that make rooms uncomfortable in winter.

Window Replacement in Covington

We handle window replacement across Covington and the Northshore, including jobs in Historic Covington, the Bedico Creek area, and neighborhoods off Hwy 21 and Hwy 190. Standard replacement jobs are single-day installs. We pull the old windows, check the rough openings for rot or moisture damage, and install the new units with proper flashing and weatherproofing.

Impact Resistant Windows for St. Tammany Parish

Impact resistant windows are a smart investment for Covington homes given the area’s exposure to Gulf storms. They also qualify for wind mitigation discounts with most Louisiana homeowner’s insurance carriers, which can offset a meaningful portion of the installation cost over time.
